Human Performance IoT Platform

NASA Johnson Space Center — EEPL (contract)

Designed and built a fleet of biometric IoT devices monitoring heart rate, core body temperature, and CO₂ within EVA suits across hostile field environments — the Neutral Buoyancy Lab (underwater, behind a DMZ, at the end of a signal-extended ethernet run), ARGOS, and DRATS analog deployments. Solved hard embedded problems: making a Bluetooth HRM work underwater, navigating network DMZs, architecting an MQTT pipeline on AWS IoT Core, and migrating to Greengrass for OTA reliability. Devices write locally for resilience, stream in parallel, and authenticate with IoT keys.

Created a 6-year longitudinal biometric dataset used by NASA flight surgeons, researchers, and mission planners. Data from this platform is actively shaping how NASA plans and executes human spaceflight.
