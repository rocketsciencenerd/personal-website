Human Performance IoT Platform

NASA Johnson Space Center — EEPL (contract)
NASA / Aerospace

Designed and built a fleet of biometric IoT devices monitoring heart rate, core body temperature, and CO₂ within EVA suits across hostile field environments — the Neutral Buoyancy Lab (underwater, behind a DMZ, at the end of a signal-extended ethernet run), ARGOS, and DRATS analog deployments. Solved hard embedded problems: making a Bluetooth HRM work underwater, navigating network DMZs, architecting an MQTT pipeline on AWS IoT Core, and migrating to Greengrass for OTA reliability. Devices write locally for resilience, stream in parallel, and authenticate with IoT keys.

Created a 6-year longitudinal biometric dataset used by NASA flight surgeons, researchers, and mission planners. Contributed as systems and data co-author across multiple peer-reviewed publications spanning ISS EVA metabolic modeling, Artemis EVA planning, core body temperature prediction, and lunar surface simulation. Data from this platform is actively shaping how NASA plans and executes human spaceflight.

AI Platform, Compliance & Enterprise Sales

Violet Labs (seed-stage)
Software Engineering

On a 3-person engineering team, built the customer-facing unified LLM provider layer integrating 7 providers (Claude, OpenAI, Bedrock, Gemini, Vertex, Groq, Azure Foundry) behind a single tool-calling interface — architected as bring-your-own-model to keep sensitive customer data out of Violet's processing agreements, enabling sales into healthcare, defense, and aerospace. Powered both the in-product agentic chat (engineers querying their hardware data in natural language) and a public MCP server giving AI clients (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Microsoft Copilot, Windsurf) secure, permission-scoped access to structured Violet workspace data. Owned the edge deployment architecture for HIPAA/FedRAMP customers. Led SOC 2 Type I + II from zero — audit-ready in 3 months, certified in 6. Re-architected CI/CD on GitHub Actions cutting build costs and deploy times by 50%.

Beyond engineering: identified that Emory University's NIH-funded cystic fibrosis research team had a data platform with no frontend — recognized the product fit, packaged the edge deployment to meet their HIPAA requirements, and pitched the CEO and CTO to pursue the deal. Closed a $10K/month contract at a company where existing contracts were $10K/year — a 12× larger deal that became the company's largest account.

Opened healthcare as a net-new vertical, set a new commercial ceiling for the company, and delivered the compliance and AI infrastructure required to sell into regulated enterprise markets.

COVID-19 Crisis Response

Johns Hopkins Hospital System
Emergency Management

Served as Planning Chief of the Unified COVID-19 Command Center during the first 90 days of the pandemic, when PPE was being rationed nationally. Emergency Infrastructure Recovery

Biobot Analytics (Series A)
Software Engineering

When Biobot's production EKS cluster — the backbone of a public health surveillance platform serving CDC, NIH, and state health departments — failed over a weekend after an untriggered Kubernetes update, immediately diagnosed the failure and put an ECS migration into motion. The cluster had been console-provisioned by a departed contractor with no IaC, no documentation, and no runbook. Designed the replacement Terraform-managed ECS Fargate architecture from scratch under pressure: networking, IAM, task definitions, and CI/CD pipelines — then executed the cutover.

Restored a production public health platform with no planned downtime window, replacing an irreproducible undocumented cluster with infrastructure that could be maintained, audited, and scaled.

Global Water Infrastructure Commissioning

GE Power — Water Engineering Leadership Program
Infrastructure

On-site engineer and sole GE representative for the commissioning of multi-million dollar water treatment facilities worldwide — writing and debugging mission-critical machine controls code (RSLogix, FactoryTalk, Modicon, Wonderware) directly on customer sites as the primary technical contact between GE, the customer, and vendors. Projects spanned municipal wastewater, industrial wastewater, drinking water, and ultra-pure water across a marquee customer list including Micron, Intel, Coca-Cola, and municipal systems — including a $240.8M upgrade to Riverside's Water Quality Control Plant (31 million gallons/day serving 320,000 people) as sole on-site engineer, and an extended deployment on the Micron Fab 10x semiconductor fabrication facility in Singapore requiring ultra-pure water systems where contamination tolerances are measured in parts per billion.

Delivered multi-million dollar projects across three continents for Fortune 500 and municipal customers. Earned three GE Above and Beyond Awards for exemplary customer service. Traveled 80% of the year including extended international deployments.
