About Me

An Engineer on a Mission.

I build software for extreme environments — biometric IoT devices inside NASA EVA suits at the Neutral Buoyancy Lab, AI platforms for regulated enterprise markets, and public health infrastructure serving CDC and NIH. When Biobot's production EKS cluster failed over a weekend, I rebuilt it in Terraform from scratch and cut over without a downtime window.

During the first 90 days of the pandemic I served as Planning Chief of the Johns Hopkins COVID-19 Command Center, coordinating a 600-person volunteer network that produced 16,000+ face shields for ICU frontline workers. Before software, I commissioned water treatment plants across three continents for GE Power — from a $240.8M municipal upgrade in California to ultra-pure water systems for Micron's semiconductor fab in Singapore.

My work at NASA has contributed to peer-reviewed publications on ISS EVA metabolic modeling, Artemis EVA planning, and lunar surface simulation. The platform I built is actively shaping how NASA plans human spaceflight.
